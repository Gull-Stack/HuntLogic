# 50-State Hunt Baseline for HuntLogic

Generated from existing HuntLogic audit work plus spot verification of official state pages. This is a medium-effort baseline for answering common hunt-planning questions, not a complete ingest inventory.

## Scope and use

- Focus: big game first, with turkey/upland/waterfowl context where it materially affects planning.
- Source posture: official agency / regulations / draw-stat pages are linked first; deeper source-tree enumeration still belongs in state-by-state ingest passes per `SOURCE-DISCOVERY-SOP.md`.
- Interpretation notes are HuntLogic shorthand for routing future product logic and user Q&A.

## Nationwide patterns worth knowing

- **Pure no-point western draws:** Alaska, Idaho, New Mexico stay strategically simple even when odds are poor.
- **Heavy point states:** Arizona, Colorado, Montana, Nevada, Oregon, Utah, Wyoming, South Dakota all require point-aware reasoning.
- **Eastern/Midwestern norm:** OTC deer/turkey with a few marquee limited-entry species (elk, moose, bear, alligator, etc.).
- **Texas / much of the South:** private-land OTC dominates; public opportunity is often handled through WMA or public-hunt drawings rather than classic western tag draws.

## Alaska (AK)

- **Agency:** Alaska Department of Fish and Game (ADF&G)
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Moose, Caribou, Dall Sheep, Mountain Goat, Brown/Grizzly Bear, Black Bear… Waterfowl and upland are major statewide opportunities under separate migratory/upland regs; turkey is not a core statewide category.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Black Bear, Sitka Black-tailed Deer, Wolf; Mixed OTC+draw: Moose, Caribou, Brown/Grizzly Bear; Draw/lottery-limited: Dall Sheep, Mountain Goat, Bison, Muskox, Roosevelt Elk
- **Points:** **None** — Pure random lottery. No preference or bonus points. Up to 6 hunt choices per species.
- **Nonresident caveats:** Quota: No general NR cap (rare hunt-specific pools exist); Fees: Moose $800, Dall Sheep $850, Brown Bear $1,000 (tags); Restrictions: NR must use licensed guide for brown bear, Dall sheep, mountain goat; NR aliens need guide for ALL species
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://www.adfg.alaska.gov/; regs/hunting hub: https://www.adfg.alaska.gov/index.cfm?adfg=wildliferegulations.hunting; draw/app portal: https://secure.wildlife.alaska.gov/ePermit; stats/results: https://www.adfg.alaska.gov/index.cfm?adfg=huntlicense.drawsupplements
- **HuntLogic note:** OTC-first with true backcountry depth; draw overlays for premium species/units.

## Arizona (AZ)

- **Agency:** Arizona Game and Fish Department (AZGFD)
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Elk, Mule Deer, Coues Deer, Pronghorn, Bighorn Sheep, Black Bear… Turkey: Turkey Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Mountain Lion; Mixed OTC+draw: Elk, Mule Deer, Coues Deer, Black Bear; Draw/lottery-limited: Pronghorn, Bighorn Sheep, Javelina, Bison, Turkey
- **Points:** **Modified Bonus Points**: 20% of tags go to highest-point applicants; 80% weighted random (points+1 entries). Loyalty points (5 consecutive years) and Hunter Ed bonus point (permanent). Separate pools per species.
- **Nonresident caveats:** Quota: 10% of total tags per hunt code (5% in bonus pass + 5% in random pass); Fees: ~4.5-5.8x resident (Elk $665 NR, Bighorn $1,815 NR); Restrictions: NR OTC archery deer limited (2,785 tags FCFS)
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://www.azgfd.com; regs/hunting hub: https://www.azgfd.com/hunting/regulations/; draw/app portal: https://draw.azgfd.com; stats/results: https://www.azgfd.com/hunting/hunt-draw-and-licenses/harvest-reporting/
- **HuntLogic note:** Draw-centric for premium species, but OTC archery/bear/lion keeps it accessible.

## Colorado (CO)

- **Agency:** Colorado Parks and Wildlife (CPW)
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Elk, Mule Deer, Whitetail Deer, Pronghorn, Black Bear, Mountain Lion… Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Whitetail Deer; Mixed OTC+draw: Elk, Pronghorn, Black Bear; Draw/lottery-limited: Mule Deer, Mountain Lion, Moose, Bighorn Sheep (RM), Desert Bighorn, Mountain Goat
- **Points:** **Three systems**: (1) Pure Preference Points for elk/deer/pronghorn/bear — highest points draw first; (2) Weighted Preference Points for moose/RM sheep/goat — 3 base + optional weighted purchases; (3) Pure Random for desert bighorn. **Major 2028 reform**: switching to 50/50 preference/bonus split for deer/elk/pronghorn/bear.
- **Nonresident caveats:** Quota: 20% (high-demand codes) to 35% (standard); standardizing to 25% in 2028; Fees: Elk $845, Deer $482-$494, Moose/Sheep/Goat $2,758 (NR); Restrictions: NR OTC archery elk eliminated west of I-25 in 2025
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://cpw.state.co.us; regs/hunting hub: https://cpw.state.co.us/rules-and-regulations; draw/app portal: https://www.cpwshop.com; stats/results: https://cpw.cvlcollections.org/items/show/159
- **HuntLogic note:** Classic mixed western state: meaningful OTC history but rapidly tightening draw structure.

## Idaho (ID)

## Montana (MT)

- **Agency:** Montana Fish, Wildlife & Parks (FWP)
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Elk, Mule Deer, Whitetail Deer, Pronghorn, Moose, Bighorn Sheep… Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Mountain Lion; Mixed OTC+draw: Elk, Mule Deer, Whitetail Deer, Black Bear; Draw/lottery-limited: Pronghorn, Moose, Bighorn Sheep, Mountain Goat, Bison
- **Points:** **Dual system**: (1) Preference Points (max 3) for NR combo licenses — 75% by points, 25% random; (2) Bonus Points (squared, unlimited) for all limited-entry permits — exponential advantage (10 pts = 101 entries). License year starts March 1.
- **Nonresident caveats:** Quota: 17,000 NR combo cap (Big Game + Elk combined); 10% per hunt code for permits; 15% landowner set-aside; Fees: Big Game Combo $1,312, Elk Combo $1,112, Moose/Sheep/Goat $1,250, Pref Point $100; Restrictions: Must draw combo before hunting general season OR applying for permits; ~2,500 fewer NR deer combos in 2026
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://fwp.mt.gov; regs/hunting hub: https://fwp.mt.gov/hunt/regulations; draw/app portal: https://fwp.mt.gov/buyandapply; stats/results: https://myfwp.mt.gov/fwpPub/drawingStatistics
- **HuntLogic note:** Hybrid system with resident-friendly general access and nonresident gatekeeping.

## Nevada (NV)

- **Agency:** Nevada Department of Wildlife (NDOW)
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Mule Deer, Elk, Pronghorn, Desert Bighorn Sheep, California Bighorn, Rocky Mtn Bighorn… Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Mountain Lion; Draw/lottery-limited: Mule Deer, Elk, Pronghorn, Desert Bighorn Sheep, California Bighorn, Rocky Mtn Bighorn…
- **Points:** **Bonus Points Squared**: (BP)² + 1 = total entries. Example: 5 points = 26 entries, 10 points = 101 entries. Points forfeited after 2 consecutive years of non-application. Separate pools per species/sex/subspecies.
- **Nonresident caveats:** Quota: ~10% of tags per hunt code (Commission Policy 24); Fees: Mule Deer $240, Elk $1,200, Sheep $1,200, Goat $1,200; Restrictions: Separate NR Guided Mule Deer Draw; RM Bighorn and Moose were resident-only in 2025
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://www.ndow.org/; regs/hunting hub: https://www.eregulations.com/nevada/hunting; draw/app portal: https://www.ndowlicensing.com; stats/results: https://www.ndow.org/blog/hunt-statistics/
- **HuntLogic note:** Pure draw/bonus state except lion; analytics-rich and highly draw-driven.

## New Mexico (NM)

- **Agency:** New Mexico Department of Game and Fish (NMDGF) — renaming to NM Dept of Wildlife under SB5
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Elk, Mule Deer, Coues Deer, Pronghorn, Bighorn Sheep, Black Bear… Turkey: Turkey Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.

## Oregon (OR)

- **Agency:** Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife (ODFW)
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Roosevelt Elk, Rocky Mtn Elk, Blacktail Deer, Mule Deer, Whitetail Deer, Pronghorn… Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Cougar; Mixed OTC+draw: Roosevelt Elk, Rocky Mtn Elk, Blacktail Deer, Mule Deer, Whitetail Deer, Black Bear; Draw/lottery-limited: Pronghorn, Bighorn Sheep, Mountain Goat
- **Points:** **75/25 Preference Points**: 75% of tags to highest-point applicants; 25% random. Drawing 1st choice resets points. 2nd-5th choice does NOT reset. Point Saver option available. Sheep/goat are pure random (no points).
- **Nonresident caveats:** Quota: 5% deer/elk, 3% pronghorn (hard statutory limits); Fees: Deer $443.50, Elk $588, Sheep/Goat $1,513.50 (NR); Restrictions: Outfitter allocation from NR pool; bear/cougar OTC same as resident
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://myodfw.com; regs/hunting hub: https://myodfw.com/articles/oregon-fishing-hunting-regulations-and-updates; draw/app portal: https://odfw.huntfishoregon.com/login; stats/results: https://myodfw.com/articles/big-game-statistics
- **HuntLogic note:** Mixed state with real general-season access and controlled upgrades; NR quotas matter.

## Utah (UT)

- **Agency:** Utah Division of Wildlife Resources (UDWR)
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Mule Deer (General), Mule Deer (LE), Elk (Any-Bull/Spike), Elk (LE Bull), Pronghorn, Moose… Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Elk (Any-Bull/Spike), Mountain Lion; Mixed OTC+draw: Black Bear; Draw/lottery-limited: Mule Deer (General), Mule Deer (LE), Elk (LE Bull), Pronghorn, Moose, Desert Bighorn…
- **Points:** **Dual system**: (1) Bonus Points (50% high-point / 50% random) for LE and OIAL; (2) Preference Points (100% by rank) for general season. Desert and RM bighorn tracked independently. Severe point creep on OIAL species.
- **Nonresident caveats:** Quota: 10% per hunt code in separate NR pool; Fees: LE Elk $1,950, Moose/Goat $3,488, Sheep $3,988, Bison $4,840 (NR, post-SB8 increases); Restrictions: Cannot hold LE + general buck deer same year; NR fees roughly doubled in 2025
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://wildlife.utah.gov/; regs/hunting hub: https://wildlife.utah.gov/hunting/hunting-regulation.html; draw/app portal: https://utahdraws.com/; stats/results: https://wildlife.utah.gov/bg-odds.html
- **HuntLogic note:** Highly structured and point-driven for premium hunting; OTC any-bull still matters.

## Washington (WA)

- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Blacktail Deer, Mule Deer, Whitetail Deer, Roosevelt Elk, Rocky Mtn Elk, Bighorn Sheep… Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.

## Iowa (IA)

- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Whitetail Deer, Elk, Black Bear Turkey: Turkey Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Whitetail Deer, Black Bear, Turkey; Draw/lottery-limited: Elk
- **Points:** None — Elk is pure random.
- **Nonresident caveats:** Quota: Elk — 1 NR or 10% (whichever greater) = 1 permit; Fees: NR elk $400; NR license $111; NR bear $151
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://dwr.virginia.gov; regs/hunting hub: https://dwr.virginia.gov/hunting/; draw/app portal: https://www.gooutdoorsvirginia.com; stats/results: Not published
- **HuntLogic note:** OTC deer/bear/turkey with one ultra-rare elk lottery.

## West Virginia (WV)

- **Agency:** West Virginia Division of Natural Resources (WVDNR)
- **Opportunity summary:** Big game: Whitetail (antlered), Whitetail (antlerless), Black Bear Turkey: Turkey Upland/waterfowl: use annual state and migratory bird regs; usually not the core draw driver in this baseline.
- **OTC vs draw:** OTC/general core: Whitetail (antlered), Black Bear, Turkey; Mixed OTC+draw: Whitetail (antlerless)
- **Points:** None.
- **Nonresident caveats:** Fees: NR Class E $119; NR bear ~$185-194 total
- **Canonical official sources:** agency: https://wvdnr.gov; regs/hunting hub: https://wvdnr.gov/hunting/; draw/app portal: https://www.wvhunt.com; stats/results: Not published
- **HuntLogic note:** OTC Appalachian state with limited-area antlerless lotteries.

## Idaho and Texas as test archetypes

- **Idaho: ready.** It is an excellent western archetype because it mixes OTC general tags and controlled hunts, has **no point system**, and exposes unusually clean official draw-odds data (`ifwis/huntplanner/odds`) plus strong species/rules navigation on IDFG. It is ideal for testing HuntLogic answers about OTC vs controlled, residency quotas, second draws, and “simple system, hard choice set” reasoning.
- **Texas: ready with one caveat.** It is an excellent non-western / private-land archetype because public drawings sit on top of a mostly private-land OTC world, the official public-hunt statistics are structured and historically deep, and species mix is broad (deer, pronghorn, exotics, sheep, turkey, hogs). The caveat is that product logic must clearly separate **public draw opportunity** from **statewide huntability on private land**, or answers will overstate draw-dependence.

## Biggest gaps before full ingestion

- Several states still lack well-published official draw-odds datasets (for example Illinois, Ohio, Minnesota, New Jersey, Virginia), so this baseline is structurally stronger on system rules than on odds math in those states.
- Waterfowl/upland source trees are only lightly represented here except where they intersect hunt-access logic; a dedicated bird-focused pass is still needed for states where migratory access is central (AR, LA, MD, NJ, NC, DE, FL, TX).
- GIS / unit-boundary links are inconsistently surfaced in the current baseline even when agencies have ArcGIS hubs; those should be added during ingestion planning.
- Some official portals are JS-heavy or bot-resistant, so parent-page enumeration may still require browser-assisted discovery in states like Montana, Utah, Washington, California, and New Jersey.
