Federal rules (USFWS for migratory birds) apply nationwide; state variations noted where significant.\n\nUse HuntLogic for personalized odds, draw strategies, and state-specific updates.\n\n## 1. Legal Methods & Equipment\n\n### Big Game (Deer, Elk, Bear, etc.)\n- **Rifles**: Centerfire rifles (.243+ for deer, .30+ for elk/bear). Lead ammo banned in CA condor zones; non-toxic required on federal lands for some species.\n- **Archery**: Compound/recerve bows (35-50 lb draw weight min.); crossbows legal in most states (age/weapon restrictions vary).\n- **Muzzleloaders**: .50 cal+ inline/percussion; saboted bullets common.\n- **Air rifles**: Legal in many states for deer (e.g., .357+ cal pre-charged pneumatic).\n- **State notes**: TX private land OTC dominates; AK requires guides for NR sheep/bear.\n\n### Upland Birds (Pheasant, Quail, Grouse)\n- Shotguns (12/20/28 ga, 2¾\" shells); #4-#8 shot (lead OK except federal refuges).\n\n### Turkey\n- Shotguns (12/20 ga, #2-#9 TSS/Hevi-Shot/lead); archery/bows; some states allow handguns/rimfire.\n\n### Waterfowl (Ducks, Geese)\n- **Non-toxic shot only** (steel #2-#BB ducks, #4 geese; Hevi-Shot/Bi Metal legal). 10-ga banned federally.\n\n**Universal**: HIP registration for migratory birds; hunter orange (400 sq in min) for big game gun seasons (not archery/waterfowl).\n\n## 2. Terrain & Habitat Basics\n\n- **Deer**: Edge habitat (fields-woods); ag lands East/South, timbered draws West.\n- **Elk**: High meadows/alpine summer; low drainages winter; aspen/oakbrush transitions.\n- **Bear**: Berries (huckleberry) summer; acorns fall; riparian spring.\n- **Upland**: Grasslands/cover for pheasant/quail; conifer ridges grouse.\n- **Turkey**: Hardwoods (oaks) strut zones; roost rivers.\n- **Waterfowl**: Marshes/X-mas trees; fields for geese; open water ducks.\n\n**State Caveats**: TX 95% private—lease access key; CA steep coastal; AK remote bush plane.\n\n## 4. Calling, Stands & Scouting Tips\n\n- **Scouting**: Trail cams, rubs/scrapes (deer), wallows (elk/bear), tracks/droppings. E-scout onX/maps pre-season.\n- **Stands**: Wind always in face; 20-30 yd shots. Treestands blinds for turkey/upland.\n- **Calling**:\n  | Species | Calls | When/How |\n  |---------|-------|----------|\n  | Deer | Grunts/bleats | Rut, soft close-range. |\n  | Elk | Bugles/cow mews | Sep rut dawn/dusk. |\n  | Turkey | Yelps/cuts | Spring locator + soft clucks. |\n  | Geese | Honks/clucks | Landed birds; flag decoys. |\n  | Ducks | Quacks/feed | Motion + blocks. |\n\nMove slow; glass first.\n\n## 5. Shot Placement & Ethical Harvest\n\n**Double Lung** priority: quick kill, trackable blood. Wait 30 min post-shot.\n- **Deer/Elk**: Broadside behind shoulder; quartering-away high.\n- **Turkey**: Head/neck (4-6\") at 40 yd max.\n- **Waterfowl**: Head/upper chest; pass-shots lead 2-4 ft.\n\n**Ethics**: Only take high-percentage shots (<40 yd archery, <300 rifle). Track diligently; no wanton waste.\n\n## 6. Processing & Field Care\n\n**Field Dress Immediately**:\n1. Cut throat, prop mouth open (drain).\n2. Gut cavity (esophagus to anus; prop open).\n3. Remove lower bowel intact.\n4. Quarter large game (elk/moose).\n\n**Cooling**: Skin if >60°F; quarter/hang 50-60°F. Ice chest birds/small game.\n**Aging**: 7-14 days 34-38°F refrig.\n**Processing**: Vacuum seal; grind trimmings.\n\n**Safety**: Trichinosis rare (bear cook 160°F); CWD avoid lymph/brain.\n\n## 7. Common Beginner Mistakes\n\n1. **Poor Planning**: Not checking regs/HIP; wrong gear/caliber.\n2. **Wind/Scent**: Hunting downwind; no play-the-wind.\n3. **Overcalling**: Constant noise spooks; less is more.\n4. **Bad Shots**: Rushed long shots; practice ethics.\n5. **Field Care Fail**: Warm gut piles spoil meat.\n6. **Access Issues**: TX private permission; public lotteries.\n7. **Timing**: Weekend crowds; hunt midweek/dawn.\n\n## Integration with HuntLogic 50-State Baseline\n\nThis mentor layer **complements** the baseline's draw/OTC focus:\n- **Baseline**: Tag access, points, quotas, odds.\n- **Mentor**: How to hunt successfully *after* the tag.\n\nCross-refs: Western draws (CO/AZ) → stand tips; East OTC → season strategy; TX private → access caveats.\n\n## Why HuntLogic is Your True Digital Mentor\n\nBeyond odds: **Personalized playbooks** blend baseline access + mentor tactics. Small Game Hunting (Squirrel, Rabbit, Dove, Quail, Pheasant, Grouse, Woodcock, Rails, Gallinules, etc.)

Small game offers accessible entry points for beginners—short seasons, abundant populations, public land opportunities. Shotguns dominate, enabling cross-over from turkey/waterfowl. **Federal migratory bird rules (USFWS) apply to dove/woodcock/rails/gallinules; HIP required.** Cross-reference HuntLogic 50-state baseline for exact dates/bags.

### Legal Methods & Equipment
- **Shotguns**: 12/20/28 ga primary (.410 OK many states); #4-#8 lead shot squirrels/rabbits/upland (non-toxic for waterfowl overlap); 2¾-3" shells.
- **Rimfire**: .22LR legal squirrels/rabbits many states (not migratory birds).
- **Archery/Airguns**: Allowed stateside for non-migratory (bows .22cal+ air rifles).
- **Dogs**: Legal upland (quail/pheasant); beagle packs rabbits.

**State variations**: Lead shot banned some refuges; no rifles pheasants MN/WI.

Check state DNR for zones (e.g., early squirrel GA Aug).

### Habitat, Scouting & Tactics
- **Squirrel**: Hardwoods (oaks/hickory); bark signs, acorns; still-hunt bark/del bark calls.
- **Rabbit**: Brushy edges/fields; jump-shoot or beagles; sit edges dawn/dusk.
- **Dove**: Fields (sunflowers/milo); decoys/pass shooting; early season roosts.
- **Quail/Pheasant**: Grasslands/CRP; walk-up/dogs; covey rise flush.
- **Grouse/Woodcock**: Young forests/wet woods; point/flush; timberdoodles zigzag.
- **Rails**: Marshes; rail walking sneak.

**Scouting**: E-scout public lands (onX); acorn masts, field edges, grit logs.

### Processing & Field Care
Pluck/breast birds; skin/quarter furred game. Cool immediately (shade/ice); age rabbits 1-2 days. Cook: Fry squirrel, dove poppers, quail whole roast.

### Beginner Mistakes
1. **Overgunning**: #6 shot squirrels; pattern test.
2. **Noise**: Crunch leaves spook; soft steps/ypsi calls.
3. **Timing**: Doves pre-noon; squirrels midday mast.
4. **Dogs untrained**: Upland chaos.
5. **Regs miss**: Migratory stamps/dates.

### State-Specific Caveats & Shotgun Cross-Advocacy
**Georgia Example**: Your turkey shotgun (#4-9 shot) loads #6 squirrels/rabbits, #7½ dove/quail—same gun hunts waterfowl (steel), turkey, small game year-round. Squirrel Aug15-Mar, dove Sep/Dec, quail Oct-Feb.

**Other States**:
- **TX**: OTC dove fields + quail private.
- **PA**: Squirrel Oct-Nov prime; grouse north.
- **MN/WI**: Pheasant Oct opener frenzy.
- **CO**: Sage grouse limited units.

**Cross-Advocacy Power**: Turkey hunter? Swap TSS #9 → #6 squirrel shells. Waterfowler? Same non-toxic loads doves/rails. Builds skills, maximizes gear—HuntLogic flags overlaps.
